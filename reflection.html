Reflection: Scratch Project – 2024

Analysis

During the Scratch project, my biggest challenge was learning how to develop an application independently without prior lessons. I struggled to structure my work because the simplicity of the task left little room for creativity. By searching, experimenting, and solving problems on my own, I developed essential skills that I could apply in other projects. This project also taught me how important it is to stay curious and determined, even when tasks seem difficult.

Insights from the POP session

In the POP session, I learned how important it is to break my work into small, achievable steps. This helps me stay focused during projects. I also discovered how reflecting on what went well and what could be improved helps me grow as a programmer.

Applying this in daily life as a programmer

I apply what I’ve learned by dividing my projects into smaller steps and setting priorities. This allows me to better plan my work and keep an overview. I also take time to reflect on what I can improve. Continuously searching for solutions independently is a skill I will keep practicing to become more self-reliant.

X-factor Reflection

  • (Em)passion: During the Scratch project, I discovered my passion for programming and game development. By persevering, I achieved my first success, which strengthened my interest in the field.
  • Entrepreneurial & Innovative: The project taught me to solve problems independently and to spot opportunities for self-improvement. Despite Scratch’s simplicity, I was able to experiment and think of creative ways to personalize my project.
  • (International) Collaboration: Although Scratch was an individual project, I realized the importance of a supportive network. In the future, I aim to collaborate more with others to exchange ideas and grow further.
  • Multi- & Disciplinarity: This project gave me a first glimpse into how different disciplines – such as logic, creativity, and user experience – come together in programming work.
  • Sustainability: Although the project was small, it was a sustainable investment in my future. The skills and mindset I developed will continue to support me in future challenges.
